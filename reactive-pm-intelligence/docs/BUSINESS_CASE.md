# Business Case

## 1. Why This Matters

Reactive maintenance is expensive, disruptive, and hard to scale. The same assets tend to fail in repeatable ways, but many operations teams do not have a reliable mechanism to convert that history into planned intervention.

Reactive to PM Intelligence addresses that exact gap.

## 2. Business Problem Statement

Common operating pain points:

- repeat failures consume technician capacity
- emergency work costs materially more than planned work
- tenant-visible outages damage confidence and SLAs
- planners lack a ranked, explainable PM backlog
- leadership lacks a quantified story linking operational signals to value creation

## 3. Solution Value Proposition

The solution turns historical work-order noise into four things leadership can use immediately:

1. portfolio hotspot visibility
2. at-risk asset prioritization
3. PM conversion opportunities
4. quantified operational and financial narrative

## 4. Expected Benefits

### Cost Benefits

- lower emergency labor and callout spend
- reduced repeat-part replacement waste
- better use of specialist technician hours

### Operational Benefits

- fewer avoidable breakdowns
- stronger maintenance planning discipline
- faster prioritization of the highest-risk assets

### Customer And Occupant Benefits

- reduced service interruption on comfort- and mobility-critical systems
- more stable facility experience
- better confidence in service delivery

### Leadership Benefits

- clearer visibility into risk concentration
- credible ROI narrative for pilot expansion
- stronger case for digital FM transformation without rip-and-replace disruption

## 5. ROI Framing

Illustrative pilot value case:

- if the top recurring patterns are converted into PM before the next failure wave
- and even a modest share of those failures are avoided
- the portfolio can reduce reactive cost, avoid visible outages, and reclaim technician capacity

Illustrative levers:

- reactive to planned conversion rate
- average emergency job cost avoided
- downtime hours prevented
- technician utilization uplift
- tenant-impact incident reduction

## 6. SLT-Level Messages

- move from break-fix firefighting to intelligence-led intervention
- use existing CAFM history as a monetizable operational asset
- create a low-regret path to predictive maintenance at portfolio scale
- improve resilience without forcing a systems replacement program

## 7. Trade-Offs

### Current Strengths

- fast pilot deployment
- high demo clarity
- explainable pattern and prediction layer
- credible execution from data to PM schedule

### Current Constraints

- heuristic model labels
- pilot-scale persistence choice
- limited workflow governance for PM publication
- synthetic data foundation prior to live account calibration

## 8. Why The Trade-Offs Are Acceptable Now

The current implementation is optimized to answer the executive question first:

```text
Is there enough value and credibility here to justify pilot expansion?
```

For that decision, speed, clarity, and explainability are more important than full enterprise hardening.

## 9. Expansion Recommendation

Recommended next step:

- expand from synthetic and demo mode into one live-account pilot

Success criteria:

- validated recurrence precision with site SMEs
- measurable PM conversion backlog
- visible reduction in repeat reactive workload on selected assets or sites
- stakeholder confidence in the dashboard and assistant workflow

## 10. Executive Conclusion

Reactive to PM Intelligence is not just an analytics demo. It is an operating model proposal: use historical maintenance data to drive preventive action, reduce avoidable failure, and create measurable financial and service impact with a clear path from pilot to production.
